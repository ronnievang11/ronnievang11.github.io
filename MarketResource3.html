HTTPS

This video really brought out the most aspect of security on the web. With every detail that she explains, from just going on a webpage, there will at least be an attacker that can already have access to that information; whether it’s through sending a private message, using credit card to purchase online items or etc. HTTPS is in term of security for websites. This video details how important it is having HTTPS and the significance that it contains. I always thought that it means more secure but never expected that there was more depth to https what it mean. I did not know that there were more properties involve in HTTPS such as identity, confidentiality, and integrity. It really surprised me how sites are able to use geolocation to have access your location from where you are searching on the web, which in a way, starting to make me feel more uncomfortable being on the web now. Another thing that surprise me was how there are well-known websites out there that are still not using HTTPS for security. She talks about the TLS connection, Transport Layer Security, in the video. At first, I did not understand what she meant by TLS and what it do, but the way how she explain it is really detailed. I find all of the stories she told in the video very interesting, especially when she told the story 3 because it talks about being the perspective of an attacker going through a website. She also counter that story with great resources and tools out there that you can use to help make your site stay secure and protective from attackers, such as SSL lab. I think that the video went really well in the way how she ended off with the last story of steps you can do in creating an HTTPS site. Especially now that HTTPS is becoming much faster, easier and cheaper than it was before.
